# MDCAT 2026 Aggregate Formula — PMC Criteria Explained

If you are applying for MBBS or BDS in Pakistan, your admission depends entirely on your **merit aggregate** — a composite score calculated from your Matric, FSc, and MDCAT scores. Here is exactly how it works.

## The PMC Merit Formula (2026)

The Pakistan Medical Commission (PMC) uses the following weightages:

| Component | Weightage |
|---|---|
| MDCAT Score | 50% |
| FSc Pre-Medical Marks | 40% |
| Matric Marks | 10% |

**Formula:**
```
Aggregate = (MDCAT / 210 × 50) + (FSc / 1100 × 40) + (Matric / 1100 × 10)
```

> Note: MDCAT 2026 is out of 210 marks. FSc and Matric are each out of 1100 for Science students.

## Step-by-Step Example

A student with:
- MDCAT: **168 / 210**
- FSc: **990 / 1100**
- Matric: **1010 / 1100**

Calculation:
- MDCAT component: (168 / 210) × 50 = **40.0**
- FSc component: (990 / 1100) × 40 = **36.0**
- Matric component: (1010 / 1100) × 10 = **9.18**

**Total Aggregate: 85.18%**

## A-Level Students

If you completed A-Levels, IBCC equivalence is required. Your A-Level marks are converted to FSc equivalent marks using the IBCC formula before being plugged into the PMC formula.

## Tips to Maximize Your Medical Aggregate

1. **MDCAT is worth 50% — it is the biggest lever.** A 10-mark improvement in MDCAT adds 2.38% to your aggregate.
2. **Every FSc mark counts.** Improving from 950 to 990 adds (40/1100 × 40 = 1.45%) to your aggregate.
