Every Pakistani university student eventually faces this question: "My transcript shows 3.5 CGPA — but this job application wants a percentage. What do I write?"

The answer is simpler than you think, but with a crucial caveat: **The formula is not universal.** While there is an "HEC Standard," individual universities often have their own specific conversion tables that they print on the back of your official transcript.

In this guide, we break down the HEC formula, university-specific variations, and why these numbers matter for your career.

---

## 1. The Standard HEC Formula (The 4.0 Scale)
The [Higher Education Commission (HEC)](https://www.hec.gov.pk) of Pakistan has established a standard conversion formula used by the majority of public and private sector universities:

> **Percentage (%) = (Obtained CGPA / Total CGPA) × 100**
> 
> *Wait, that's the simple way. The **Standard HEC Multiplyer** is:*
> 
> **Percentage = CGPA × 25**

This formula assumes a linear distribution where a 4.0 is 100% and a 2.0 is 50%. This is the formula accepted by most government organizations and [FPSC](https://www.fpsc.gov.pk)/[PPSC](https://www.ppsc.gop.pk) applications.

### Examples:
- CGPA of **3.5** → 3.5 × 25 = **87.5%**
- CGPA of **3.0** → 3.0 × 25 = **75%**

### 2. Scholarships
HEC Indigenous scholarships, PEEF, and the [PM Laptop Scheme](/guides/pm-laptop-scheme-2026-guide) express eligibility in CGPA (usually 2.5+ or 3.0+), but some private sector scholarships ask for percentage.

### 3. International Graduate Programs
If you are applying to universities abroad, US schools use GPA (they will re-evaluate yours), but UK/Canadian programs often ask for percentage. Use the standard formula unless your uni provides an official WES conversion.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is CGPA × 25 official?
It is the widely accepted HEC-endorsed standard formula. However, always check the back of your transcript. Many universities like [NUST](https://nust.edu.pk) or [FAST](https://nu.edu.pk) include their own percentage equivalent.

### What if my transcript already shows percentage?
Perfect — use that number directly. Some universities (like BZU or PU) issue dual-format transcripts.

### Can I negotiate a higher percentage with my university?
If your university uses a non-linear grading curve, the CGPA × 25 formula may underrepresent your actual performance. You can request a "Percentage Certificate" from your Controller of Examinations.
