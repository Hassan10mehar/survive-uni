Getting a fully funded scholarship to study abroad is the dream for many Pakistani students. However, the application process can be daunting. For the 2026 academic cycle, several prestigious global programs are opening their doors.

In this guide, we break down the top international scholarships available for Pakistani citizens, covering eligibility, benefits, and deadlines.

## Top Fully Funded Scholarships for 2026

### 1. The Fulbright Scholarship (USA)
The Fulbright Program is one of the most famous scholarships in the world. For Pakistanis, it's managed by the USEFP. It covers tuition, airfare, a living stipend, and health insurance for Master's and PhD programs.

*   **Degree Levels:** Master's, PhD.
*   **Benefits:** Full funding (everything covered).
*   **Typical Deadline:** May 2026 (for 2027 start).
*   **Key Requirement:** GRE General Score.

### 2. Chevening Scholarship (UK)
Chevening is the UK government’s global scholarship program. It is awarded to individuals with strong academic backgrounds who also demonstrate leadership potential.

*   **Degree Levels:** One-year Master's.
*   **Benefits:** Tuition fees, monthly stipend, travel costs.
*   **Typical Deadline:** November 2025 (for 2026 start).
*   **Key Requirement:** 2 years of work experience (can be internships).

## Tips for a Winning Application

1.  **Start Early:** Most global scholarships require at least 6-12 months of preparation.
2.  **Focus on the Personal Statement:** This is where you tell your story. Why do you deserve this? How will you help Pakistan after returning?
3.  **Get Strong Recommendations:** Ask professors or supervisors who actually know your work.

## FAQ

### Can I apply for scholarships with a low CGPA?
Yes, some scholarships (like Turkiye Burslari or CSC) are more holistic. However, for Fulbright or Chevening, a strong academic record is usually expected. Check our [GPA Calculator](/tools/gpa-calculator) to track your progress.

### Do I need IELTS for every scholarship?
Not necessarily. Many Chinese and Turkish scholarships don't require it if your previous degree was in English. However, for UK/USA, it's almost always mandatory.
